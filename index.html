Technical Details

Detailed Problem & Vision
Traditional vital sign monitoring in standard hospital wards often relies on spot-checking by nursing staff, leading to dangerous gaps in data where patient deterioration can go unnoticed. While ICUs have continuous monitors, these are expensive, bulky, and generate a high rate of "dumb alarms" (false positives) causing alarm fatigue.

Vita's Vision: To bring ICU-level continuous monitoring to standard wards and remote clinics using cost-effective embedded systems (Edge Computing). By storing data locally and analyzing it in real-time, Vita aims to provide "Intelligent Alarms" that not only alert staff but provide context.
Comprehensive Signal Acquisition
The device is designed to measure 5 critical parameters: ECG, SpO2 (Oxygen), Pulse (Heart Rate), Temperature, and Blood Pressure.

AD8232 (ECG): Amplifies microvolt-level electrical heart signals while filtering out noise.
MAX30102 (SpO2 & Pulse): Emits Red and IR light into the skin to calculate oxygen saturation and heart pulse rhythm.
DS18B20 (Temperature): Digital precision thermal sensor for continuous core body temperature tracking.
Blood Pressure (Cuffless): Calculated intelligently using an advanced mathematical equation based on the synchronization of ECG and PPG signals (Pulse Transit Time), eliminating the need for a bulky physical cuff.
Processing Architecture
The ADC Bottleneck: The Raspberry Pi is a powerful microcomputer, but lacks a native Hardware Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC). To solve this, our architecture utilizes an external ADC module (or an Arduino acting as an intermediate node) to convert analog voltages into digital values.

Raspberry Pi Core: The digital data is sent to the Pi via I2C/Serial communication. The Pi runs Python-based backend scripts to log this time-series data into a local SQLite/MySQL database, ensuring no data is lost even if the network disconnects.
